Why Sequential Beats Simultaneous

Fire the §40.3.2 B5 termination notice on May 1. Send a cover letter the same day pre-signaling the densification submittal. File the full §10 alterations package two to three weeks later — after NBIX finishes internal analysis and approves costs.

  • Inverts the §10 reasonableness analysis. With no B5 growth path, any "parking / capacity" objection becomes pretextual
  • NBIX is Sole Occupant (§1.4) — narrows Gemdale's universe of "reasonable" objections to code and Building Systems only
  • Architect-sealed drawings + City plan check removes Gemdale's code-review authority (the AHJ adjudicates, not the landlord)
  • MEP calcs pre-answer Building Systems objections — HVAC, electrical load, life safety occupant count
  • If Gemdale obstructs anyway — that behavior becomes evidence of landlord bad faith, ammunition for Decision 3

Downside Concerns — Every One Addressed

Concerns Addressed
"What if Gemdale drags out their response indefinitely?"§10's "shall not be... delayed" standard gives NBIX a breach claim after a reasonable period (typically 30 days). The submittal cover letter will explicitly request response within 30 days and reserve rights.
"What if Gemdale conditions consent on something unrelated?"Unrelated conditioning is textbook unreasonable. "You can densify if you also exercise B5" is the exact kind of pretext §10 is designed to block.
"If Gemdale refuses, do we proceed anyway?"Not without Cooley. But NBIX's options include specific performance, monetary damages, and offset against rent. The stronger play is specific performance — a ruling that the denial was unreasonable is itself evidence in Decision 3.

Anticipated Gemdale Objections — Pre-Answered

"Parking will be inadequate at higher density"
§1.7.1 grants NBIX exclusive parking as Sole Occupant. Run the math against the lease ratio. If density stays within ratio, there is no lease violation. Self-defeating argument: B5 would have added more parking demand than densification does.
"This violates building code"
Gemdale has no independent code-review authority. NBIX hires a licensed California architect, files for plan check with City of San Diego. The Authority Having Jurisdiction adjudicates. Any "code violation" claim without an actual plan-check denial is pretextual on its face.
"Building Systems can't handle it"
The only place Gemdale has a legitimate foothold. MEP engineer runs the calcs and either demonstrates existing capacity covers it, or scopes the upgrade work with NBIX as payor. Once on paper, Gemdale has nothing to object to.
"You need to exercise B5 instead"
§10 explicitly protects against conditioning consent on unrelated matters. This is textbook unreasonable conditioning — not just pretext, but the kind that creates a durable breach claim.
"We need more time / more studies"
§10's "shall not be... delayed" applies. Any further delay request must be specific and objectively justified. Vague "we're reviewing" after 30 days is itself breach.

The Mechanics — How It Works

Partner acquires the Invesco mezz at par ($100M) instead of letting King Street refinance it. Partner acquires or controls the OZK senior. Partner then offers Gemdale a deed-in-lieu + release from OZK personal guarantees. If Gemdale refuses, UCC Article 9 non-judicial foreclosure on the mezz. NBIX's absolute NNN lease survives foreclosure.

  • Gemdale gets $0 — no reward for being a blocking party. Their equity is illusion ($0–30M against a depressed campus value)
  • Decisions 1 and 2 depress their narrative — no growth premium, no expansion story, densification confirms stabilized-only thesis
  • Any §10 obstruction in Decision 2 becomes evidence of landlord bad faith — reinforces creditor rationale
  • 90–120 day timeline vs. 6–9 months for negotiation or 12–24+ for foreclosure-then-bankruptcy
  • Partner basis ~$540–575M (post-squeeze) — lower than original $575M estimate because Decisions 1 and 2 do the work
  • Kurt's question tomorrow: "Will Invesco sell at par to our partner instead of to King Street?" Invesco is indifferent to counterparty

How the Squeeze Works

Two moves, one outcome. B5 termination strips the growth premium out of the campus story. Densification confirms NBIX solves growth internally, locking in a "stabilized NNN only" narrative that caps the cap rate.

The result: Gemdale's campus value is pushed from $600M+ toward ~$540–575M — exactly the range where a partner can acquire the debt stack at an attractive basis and still offer NBIX favorable long-term terms.
