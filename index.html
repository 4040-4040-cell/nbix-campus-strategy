Terminate the Building 5 Expansion Option

Exercise §40.3.2 sole-discretion termination right. Send notice by May 1, ahead of densification submittal. ROFR, ROFO, and renewals remain intact.

Tenant exercises sole discretion under §40.3.2 to terminate the Expansion Option. Saves ~$297K (2 months ground rent at $148.5K/mo). Holdback expires earlier of 12 months after notice or June 30, 2027. Sequence matters — firing this first removes Gemdale's commercial motive to obstruct the densification submittal that follows two weeks later.

Why This Is the Right Move

  • Eliminates $68–73M NPV auto-extension liability — exercising B5 would extend all existing phases by 41–44 months (§40.2.4)
  • Stops non-creditable ground rent ($1.78M/yr flowing to Gemdale with zero offset)
  • Removes Gemdale's incentive to block densification — with no B5 path to funnel NBIX toward, obstruction becomes landlord harassment with §10 breach exposure
  • Depresses campus valuation by stripping the growth premium — reinforces partner basis math in Decision 3
  • Weakens Gemdale's NOI narrative to OZK ($1.78M/yr gone) — accelerates creditor-driven resolution
  • Under post-acquisition ownership, NBIX retains full ability to build on Lot 5 at its own timing and cost basis
Downside Concerns — Every One Addressed
"Do we lose the right to expand onto Lot 5 forever?" No. Termination only ends Gemdale's landlord-build mechanism. Post-acquisition, NBIX retains full entitlement to build on Lot 5 at NBIX's cost basis and timing — materially better economics than a Gemdale build-to-suit.
"What if densification doesn't give us enough capacity?" Decision 5 scenarios handle residual gap on better economics than B5 would deliver. Densification buys 3–5 years of runway while ownership resolves and Phase 3 data reads out.
"Does this weaken Gemdale's posture with King Street?" Yes — intentionally. Weakening Gemdale's NOI and valuation story is the point. Decision 3 Branch B depends on it.

RECOMMENDED · SEQUENTIAL PLAY

NBIX-Led Internal Densification

B5 termination first. Densification submittal second (mid-May). Gemdale receives the package with their leverage already gone.

Fire the §40.3.2 B5 termination notice on May 1. Send a cover letter the same day pre-signaling the densification submittal. File the full §10 alterations package two to three weeks later — after NBIX finishes internal analysis and approves costs.

Why Sequential Beats Simultaneous

  • Inverts the §10 reasonableness analysis. With no B5 growth path, any "parking / capacity" objection becomes pretextual
  • NBIX is Sole Occupant (§1.4) — narrows Gemdale's universe of "reasonable" objections to code and Building Systems only
  • Architect-sealed drawings + City plan check removes Gemdale's code-review authority (the AHJ adjudicates, not the landlord)
  • MEP calcs pre-answer Building Systems objections — HVAC, electrical load, life safety occupant count
  • If Gemdale obstructs anyway — that behavior becomes evidence of landlord bad faith, ammunition for Decision 3
Downside Concerns — Every One Addressed
"What if Gemdale drags out their response indefinitely?" §10's "shall not be... delayed" standard gives NBIX a breach claim after a reasonable period (typically 30 days). The submittal cover letter will explicitly request response within 30 days and reserve rights.
"What if Gemdale conditions consent on something unrelated?" Unrelated conditioning is textbook unreasonable. "You can densify if you also exercise B5" is the exact kind of pretext §10 is designed to block.
"If Gemdale refuses, do we proceed anyway?" Not without Cooley. But NBIX's options include specific performance, monetary damages, and offset against rent. The stronger play is specific performance — a ruling that the denial was unreasonable is itself evidence in Decision 3.

Anticipated Gemdale Objections — Pre-Answered

"Parking will be inadequate at higher density"
§1.7.1 grants NBIX exclusive parking as Sole Occupant. Run the math against the lease ratio. If density stays within ratio, there is no lease violation. Self-defeating argument: B5 would have added more parking demand than densification does.
"This violates building code"
Gemdale has no independent code-review authority. NBIX hires a licensed California architect, files for plan check with City of San Diego. The Authority Having Jurisdiction adjudicates. Any "code violation" claim without an actual plan-check denial is pretextual on its face.
"Building Systems can't handle it"
The only place Gemdale has a legitimate foothold. MEP engineer runs the calcs and either demonstrates existing capacity covers it, or scopes the upgrade work with NBIX as payor. Once on paper, Gemdale has nothing to object to.
"You need to exercise B5 instead"
§10 explicitly protects against conditioning consent on unrelated matters. This is textbook unreasonable conditioning — not just pretext, but the kind that creates a durable breach claim.
"We need more time / more studies"
§10's "shall not be... delayed" applies. Any further delay request must be specific and objectively justified. Vague "we're reviewing" after 30 days is itself breach.
RECOMMENDED · CHAD'S WORKAROUND

Creditor-Driven Extinguishment

Don't negotiate with Gemdale equity — replace their creditors and force deed-in-lieu. Gemdale gets $0. Timeline: 90–120 days.

Partner acquires the Invesco mezz at par ($100M) instead of letting King Street refinance it. Partner acquires or controls the OZK senior. Partner then offers Gemdale a deed-in-lieu + release from OZK personal guarantees. If Gemdale refuses, UCC Article 9 non-judicial foreclosure on the mezz. NBIX's absolute NNN lease survives foreclosure.

Why This Path Wins

  • Gemdale gets $0 — no reward for being a blocking party. Their equity is illusion ($0–30M against a depressed campus value)
  • Decisions 1 and 2 depress their narrative — no growth premium, no expansion story, densification confirms stabilized-only thesis
  • Any §10 obstruction in Decision 2 becomes evidence of landlord bad faith — reinforces creditor rationale
  • 90–120 day timeline vs. 6–9 months for negotiation or 12–24+ for foreclosure-then-bankruptcy
  • Partner basis ~$540–575M (post-squeeze) — lower than original $575M estimate because Decisions 1 and 2 do the work
  • Kurt's question tomorrow: "Will Invesco sell at par to our partner instead of to King Street?" Invesco is indifferent to counterparty
  • Partners in sequence: Breakthrough (warm, primary) · BioMed · Healthpeak · Alexandria
Downside Concerns — Every One Addressed
"Doesn't this require Gemdale's cooperation?" No — Gemdale accepts because the alternative is worse: UCC Article 9 foreclosure that crystallizes OZK personal guarantees. The release-from-guarantee is the carrot; foreclosure is the stick.
"What if Gemdale files Chapter 11?" Mezz foreclosure doesn't trigger senior default and doesn't automatically draw bankruptcy. The mezz UCC Art. 9 path was confirmed non-judicial in 2025. The NNN lease survives foreclosure.
"Does OZK actually hold personal guarantees?" Load-bearing question — Cooley confirms Thursday. If yes, Branch B proceeds as designed. If no, we need a different pressure point.

HORIZONTAL IMPACT

Soleno $2.9B Acquisition — Cross-Cutting Effects

The Soleno acquisition touches every decision in the tree. It is not a separate decision — it is a condition that changes the math on all five.

Impact by Decision

Decision 1 — B5 Termination

Confirms Aperture as anchor. Soleno integration will be absorbed into existing campus — reinforces the case that B5 is unnecessary overhead.

Decision 2 — Densification

Headcount ramp from Soleno must overlay the densification baseline. Accelerates the urgency of the §10 submittal.

Decision 3 — Ownership

Cash drain from the $2.9B acquisition favors non-cash structures. Strengthens the case for creditor-driven path (partner-funded) over direct purchase.

Decision 4 — Partner Selection

Soleno's pipeline additions make Aperture more attractive to partners — stronger NOI story, longer lease duration implied by growth.

Decision 5 — Expansion

Accelerates expansion trigger. Inherited Soleno facilities may provide interim capacity, but long-term integration favors campus consolidation.
